# Warm Signal — Design Philosophy

Translation is the act of bridging. Two voices, one meaning. The icon does not illustrate language — it embodies the structural relationship between source and echo, between what was said and what is understood. Form follows this invisible architecture.

The palette is warm amber-to-ember: not the cold blue of machines, but the temperature of a lamp in a reading room. Gradient moves top to bottom, as text moves down a page, as meaning flows from original to translation. The warmth is intentional and earned — it signals safety, locality, something happening on your device rather than in a distant server room.

The symbol is two horizontal bars, placed with painstaking care. The upper bar, slightly shorter and softer, is the source — the original voice. The lower bar, wider and fully opaque, is the translation — the response, the echo made visible. The asymmetry is precise: not random, but calibrated so the relationship reads instantly. The gap between them is not empty space but a breath, the moment of translation itself. Every pixel of these proportions was labored over.

Space is governed by restraint. The rounded square container uses a radius that belongs to this specific device era — not too soft, not too architectural. Padding is generous, letting the symbol breathe without floating. The icon must hold at 128 pixels with museum quality and remain legible at 16 pixels as a single recognizable silhouette. This demands master-level execution: shapes that simplify gracefully, not accidentally.

No text enters the icon. No globe, no speech bubble, no arrow. Those are shorthand for tools that explain themselves. This mark does not explain — it *is*. The meticulous construction of two bars in precise geometric relationship, rendered on warm amber, constitutes the entire argument.
